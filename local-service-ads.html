Common Questions

Google Local Service Ads FAQ

Google Local Service Ads (LSAs) are a pay-per-lead ad format designed specifically for local service businesses. Unlike traditional Google Ads where you pay per click, LSAs only charge you when a customer directly calls or messages your business through the ad. They appear at the very top of Google's search results — above traditional ads, Maps results, and organic listings — making them the most prominent spot on the page.

Google Local Service Ads are available for a wide range of local service categories including HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing, landscaping, pest control, locksmithing, cleaning services, real estate agents, lawyers, financial planners, and more. Google periodically expands the eligible categories. If you're unsure whether your business qualifies, we'll tell you in your free audit call.

LSA costs vary by industry and location. You set a weekly budget and Google charges you per lead — typically ranging from $15 to $150+ per lead depending on your service type and market competitiveness. The beauty of the model is that you control the budget ceiling entirely, and we actively dispute bad leads to make sure you're only paying for contacts that have real potential. Our management fee is separate from your ad spend and is based on your campaign size.

The Google Guaranteed verification process involves a background check on your business and license verification. In most cases, the process takes 2–4 weeks from start to finish. We handle all of the paperwork and guide you through every step so there are no delays caused by missing documents or missed deadlines. Once approved, your ads can start generating leads within days.

Yes — and many of our clients do exactly that. LSAs and Google Ads serve different functions. LSAs are pay-per-lead and appear at the very top for high-intent local searches. Traditional Google Ads give you more control over keywords, targeting, and creative, and can capture a wider funnel. Running both together creates a dominant presence across the entire Google results page. We can manage both for you and make sure the budgets are working in harmony.

Google allows you to dispute leads that are outside your service area, for a service you don't offer, spam calls, or wrong numbers. When managed correctly, disputes can recover a meaningful portion of your ad spend. This is one of the biggest advantages of having an experienced agency manage your LSAs — most business owners either don't dispute leads at all or don't do it correctly. We handle all disputes on your behalf as part of our standard management service.
